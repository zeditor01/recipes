About Us

Recipes and Worked Examples for Getting Things Done in z/OS.

"Zeditor" is dedicated to providing recipes and worked examples on how to achieve specific tasks with z/OS.

It does not attempt to cover the breadth and depth of information that is published in the comprehensive product documentation in the IBM Knowledge Center. It is intended as a repository of limited-scope examples and recipes for "getting things done" in z/OS.

All the content is served from public github repositories. The documentation is written in github markdown. Associated code samples are stored in the same github repository.

We are all employed by IBM Australia as System Z technical specialists, and share a passion for building things on our z/OS test rigs. (we all use IBM Z Development and Test software to run z/OS systems as a virtualized guest of Linux on some beefy x64 systems at home).

Our z/OS test rigs are what we use for customer demonstrations and self education. As we work with clients to deploy z/OS software in real world environments, we apply practical experiences from the real world to enhance our demonstration platforms. As we grow our experiences, we document them for future reference, and include some of those lessons learnt that you just don't seem to get in the formal product documentation.

As our recipe notebooks for getting things done grew larger and larger, we wanted a permenant place to safely store them and share them. Github was the obvious repository for our code and documentation. This website will grow over time, so please drop by from time to time to find new recipes.

Everything on this site is free to access anonymously. You may not reproduce the material without permission from the copyright owner.

The primary purpose of this site is to share worked examples that might help others anywhere in the world. Your local IBM team is the best place to discuss your IT requirements. However, we do welcome feedback about our recipes and articles. If you wish to contact us, please email the author of the article you are referring to, using "Zeditor Feedback" as the subject of your email.
